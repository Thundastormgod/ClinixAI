# Essential Drug Interactions Reference

## High-Risk Drug Combinations

### Combinations to NEVER Use Together

#### ACE Inhibitors + Potassium-Sparing Diuretics
- **Drugs**: Lisinopril, Enalapril + Spironolactone, Amiloride
- **Risk**: Severe hyperkalemia (high potassium)
- **Symptoms**: Muscle weakness, irregular heartbeat
- **Action**: Avoid combination or monitor potassium closely

#### Warfarin + NSAIDs
- **Drugs**: Warfarin + Ibuprofen, Aspirin, Diclofenac
- **Risk**: Severe bleeding, GI hemorrhage
- **Action**: Use paracetamol instead for pain

#### MAOIs + Many Drugs
- **MAOIs**: Phenelzine, Tranylcypromine
- **Avoid**: SSRIs, tyramine-rich foods, decongestants
- **Risk**: Serotonin syndrome, hypertensive crisis
- **Action**: Wait 2 weeks between switching

#### Methotrexate + NSAIDs
- **Risk**: Methotrexate toxicity (bone marrow suppression)
- **Action**: Avoid combination, use paracetamol

#### Simvastatin + Certain Antibiotics
- **Antibiotics**: Erythromycin, Clarithromycin
- **Risk**: Rhabdomyolysis (muscle breakdown)
- **Symptoms**: Muscle pain, dark urine, weakness
- **Action**: Stop statin temporarily during antibiotic course

## Common Interactions by Drug Class

### Antimalarials

#### Artemether-Lumefantrine (Coartem)
- **Take with**: Fatty food (increases absorption)
- **Avoid**: Grapefruit juice
- **Interactions**: 
  - Reduces effect of hormonal contraceptives
  - Increased toxicity with QT-prolonging drugs

#### Quinine
- **Interactions**:
  - Increases digoxin levels
  - Risk of hypoglycemia (monitor blood sugar)
  - Avoid with mefloquine (seizure risk)

### Antibiotics

#### Metronidazole
- **Avoid**: Alcohol (severe nausea, vomiting - disulfiram reaction)
- **Wait**: 48 hours after completing course before drinking
- **Also avoid**: Warfarin (increased bleeding risk)

#### Ciprofloxacin (Fluoroquinolones)
- **Avoid taking with**: Antacids, calcium, iron, zinc (reduces absorption)
- **Space**: 2 hours before or 6 hours after these supplements
- **Increases levels of**: Theophylline, caffeine, warfarin

#### Doxycycline
- **Avoid with**: Antacids, calcium, iron, dairy
- **Take**: 1 hour before or 2 hours after meals
- **Increases**: Sun sensitivity (use sunscreen)

#### Rifampicin (TB treatment)
- **Major inducer**: Reduces effectiveness of MANY drugs
- **Affected drugs**: 
  - Hormonal contraceptives (use additional method)
  - Warfarin (need higher doses)
  - HIV medications (dose adjustments needed)
  - Antifungals
  - Steroids

### HIV Medications

#### General Principles
- **Timing critical**: Many ARVs must be taken at specific times
- **Food requirements**: Some with food, some without
- **Drug interactions**: Very common - always check

#### Efavirenz
- **Take**: On empty stomach, at bedtime
- **Avoid**: Alcohol (increases side effects)
- **Interactions**: Reduces effect of hormonal contraceptives

#### Nevirapine
- **Interactions**: Similar to Efavirenz
- **Monitoring**: Liver function tests important

### Pain Medications

#### Paracetamol (Acetaminophen)
- **Max dose**: 4g/day in adults, 3g/day in elderly
- **Liver risk**: Avoid with heavy alcohol use
- **Safe in**: Pregnancy, kidney disease

#### NSAIDs (Ibuprofen, Diclofenac)
- **Avoid in**:
  - Active bleeding
  - Peptic ulcer disease
  - Kidney disease
  - Last trimester of pregnancy
  - With ACE inhibitors (kidney damage)
  - With anticoagulants (bleeding)
- **Take with**: Food to protect stomach

#### Tramadol
- **Serotonin risk**: With SSRIs, MAOIs
- **Seizure risk**: In epilepsy or with other seizure-lowering drugs
- **Respiratory risk**: With other sedatives

### Diabetes Medications

#### Metformin
- **Hold before**: CT scan with contrast (restart after 48h if kidneys OK)
- **Avoid with**: Heavy alcohol (lactic acidosis risk)
- **Take with**: Food (reduces GI upset)

#### Sulfonylureas (Glibenclamide, Glipizide)
- **Hypoglycemia risk increases with**:
  - Alcohol
  - Beta-blockers (mask symptoms)
  - NSAIDs
  - Sulfonamide antibiotics

### Cardiovascular

#### Digoxin
- **Narrow therapeutic range**: Toxicity common
- **Levels increased by**:
  - Amiodarone
  - Verapamil
  - Quinidine
  - Clarithromycin
- **Toxicity signs**: Nausea, yellow vision, irregular heartbeat

#### Amiodarone
- **Prolongs QT**: Risk with other QT-prolonging drugs
- **Multiple interactions**: Check everything
- **Monitoring**: Thyroid, liver, lungs

## Food-Drug Interactions

### Take WITH Food
- NSAIDs (protects stomach)
- Metformin (reduces nausea)
- Artemether-Lumefantrine (fatty food)

### Take WITHOUT Food
- Tetracyclines (1hr before or 2hr after meals)
- Fluoroquinolones (2hr before/6hr after dairy/antacids)
- Efavirenz (on empty stomach)

### Avoid Grapefruit
- Statins (especially simvastatin)
- Some calcium channel blockers
- Immunosuppressants
- Some antihistamines

### Avoid Alcohol
- Metronidazole (48hr after completion)
- Paracetamol (high doses)
- Sedatives
- Many psychiatric medications

## Special Populations

### Pregnancy
- **Avoid**: Warfarin, ACE inhibitors, tetracyclines, methotrexate
- **Safe**: Paracetamol, most penicillins, many cephalosporins

### Breastfeeding
- **Avoid**: Metronidazole (high doses), codeine, ergotamine
- **Usually safe**: Paracetamol, ibuprofen (short term), penicillins

### Kidney Disease
- **Dose reduce**: Most drugs cleared by kidneys
- **Avoid**: NSAIDs, high-dose metformin
- **Monitor**: Digoxin, aminoglycosides

### Liver Disease
- **Careful with**: Paracetamol (lower max dose), most drugs
- **Avoid**: Methotrexate, statins in severe disease

## Quick Reference - Common OTC Interactions

### Antacids
- **Reduce absorption of**: Many antibiotics, iron, HIV drugs
- **Space**: 2 hours apart from other medications

### Herbal Products
- **St. John's Wort**: Reduces effect of MANY drugs (contraceptives, HIV drugs, warfarin)
- **Ginkgo**: Increases bleeding risk
- **Garlic supplements**: Increases bleeding risk
