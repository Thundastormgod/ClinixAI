# Common Symptom Assessment Guide

## Fever Assessment

### Definition
- Normal temperature: 36.5°C - 37.5°C
- Low-grade fever: 37.5°C - 38°C
- Moderate fever: 38°C - 39°C
- High fever: 39°C - 40°C
- Very high fever: >40°C (Danger sign)

### Duration Categories
- Acute fever: <7 days
- Prolonged fever: 7-14 days
- Chronic fever: >14 days

### Red Flags with Fever
- Stiff neck (meningitis)
- Severe headache with photophobia
- Rash that doesn't blanch (petechial)
- Altered consciousness
- Convulsions
- Very high fever >40°C
- Fever in infant <3 months

### Common Causes by Region (Africa)
1. **Malaria** - Consider in all febrile patients in endemic areas
2. **Typhoid fever** - Prolonged fever, abdominal symptoms
3. **Respiratory infections** - Cough, cold symptoms
4. **Urinary tract infection** - Dysuria, frequency
5. **Dengue** - In endemic areas, with rash and body aches

## Headache Assessment

### Red Flag Headaches (Emergency)
- "Thunderclap" headache - sudden severe onset
- Fever + stiff neck
- New headache in HIV patient
- Headache after head injury
- Progressive worsening over days/weeks
- Associated with weakness or vision changes
- Headache waking patient from sleep

### Common Types
1. **Tension headache**: Band-like, both sides, mild-moderate
2. **Migraine**: One side, throbbing, nausea, light sensitivity
3. **Cluster headache**: Severe, around eye, tearing
4. **Sinusitis headache**: Facial pain, worse bending forward

## Abdominal Pain Assessment

### Location-Based Assessment

#### Right Upper Quadrant
- Liver problems
- Gallbladder disease
- Right kidney issues

#### Epigastric (Upper Middle)
- Gastritis
- Peptic ulcer
- Pancreatitis

#### Left Upper Quadrant
- Spleen problems
- Left kidney issues
- Gastritis

#### Right Lower Quadrant
- Appendicitis (classic location)
- Ovarian problems (females)
- Ectopic pregnancy

#### Left Lower Quadrant
- Diverticulitis
- Ovarian problems (females)
- Constipation

### Red Flags in Abdominal Pain
- Rigid, board-like abdomen
- Severe pain with vomiting blood
- Fever + severe abdominal pain
- Signs of shock (fast pulse, cold extremities)
- Pain radiating to back (pancreatitis, aortic aneurysm)
- Absent bowel sounds
- Blood in stool or black stools

## Cough Assessment

### Duration
- Acute cough: <3 weeks
- Subacute: 3-8 weeks
- Chronic: >8 weeks

### Productive vs Dry
- **Productive (wet)**: Pneumonia, bronchitis, TB
- **Dry**: Viral infection, asthma, ACE inhibitor side effect

### Red Flags with Cough
- Coughing blood (hemoptysis)
- Severe shortness of breath
- High fever
- Weight loss + night sweats (TB suspicion)
- Chest pain with breathing
- Cough >3 weeks (rule out TB in endemic areas)

### TB Screening Questions
- Cough >2 weeks?
- Night sweats?
- Unexplained weight loss?
- Contact with TB patient?
- HIV positive?

## Diarrhea Assessment

### Types
- **Acute watery**: Usually viral, risk of dehydration
- **Bloody (dysentery)**: Bacterial, needs antibiotics
- **Persistent**: >14 days, needs investigation

### Dehydration Assessment
| Sign | None | Some | Severe |
|------|------|------|--------|
| General condition | Well, alert | Restless, irritable | Lethargic/unconscious |
| Eyes | Normal | Sunken | Very sunken |
| Thirst | Drinks normally | Thirsty, drinks eagerly | Unable to drink |
| Skin pinch | Goes back quickly | Goes back slowly | Goes back very slowly (>2s) |

### Red Flags in Diarrhea
- Signs of severe dehydration
- Blood in stool
- High fever
- Severe abdominal pain
- Unable to keep fluids down
- Very young (<6 months) or malnourished child

## Chest Pain Assessment

### Cardiac Warning Signs (EMERGENCY)
- Crushing central chest pain
- Pain radiating to left arm, jaw, or back
- Associated with sweating, nausea
- Shortness of breath
- Known heart disease + new chest pain

### Other Causes
- **Pleuritic** (sharp, worse with breathing): Pneumonia, pleurisy
- **Musculoskeletal**: Tender to touch, related to movement
- **Gastric**: Burning, related to meals
- **Anxiety**: With palpitations, tingling

## Skin Rash Assessment

### Red Flags
- Rash + fever + unwell (consider meningococcal)
- Non-blanching rash (petechiae/purpura)
- Blistering affecting large areas
- Rash + difficulty breathing
- Rapidly spreading red/warm skin (cellulitis)

### Common Rashes
1. **Measles**: Fever → rash (head to feet), Koplik spots
2. **Chickenpox**: Itchy blisters in different stages
3. **Scabies**: Itchy, especially at night, finger webs
4. **Ringworm**: Circular, scaly, central clearing
5. **Eczema**: Dry, itchy, flexural areas

## Joint Pain Assessment

### Red Flags
- Hot, swollen, red joint (septic arthritis emergency)
- Multiple joint swelling + fever (rheumatic fever)
- Joint pain + skin rash + fever
- Inability to bear weight
- Recent injury with deformity

### Common Causes
- **Osteoarthritis**: Older age, weight-bearing joints
- **Rheumatoid**: Morning stiffness, multiple joints
- **Gout**: Sudden severe pain, often big toe
- **Reactive**: After infection (STI, diarrhea)
