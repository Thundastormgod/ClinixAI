# Medical Red Flags - Warning Signs Requiring Immediate Action

## CRITICAL - Seek Emergency Care Immediately

### Airway & Breathing Emergencies
- **Cannot breathe or severe difficulty breathing**
- Choking or obstructed airway
- Blue lips or tongue (cyanosis)
- Gasping or very slow breathing
- Stridor (high-pitched sound when breathing in)
- Severe wheezing not responding to treatment

### Cardiovascular Emergencies
- **Crushing chest pain** (especially with sweating, nausea)
- Sudden severe weakness on one side of body (stroke)
- Sudden confusion or difficulty speaking (stroke)
- Cold, pale limbs with weak or absent pulse
- Massive bleeding that won't stop
- Signs of shock: pale, cold, sweaty, rapid weak pulse

### Neurological Emergencies
- **Unconscious or unresponsive**
- Active seizure/convulsion
- Sudden severe headache ("worst headache of my life")
- Stiff neck with fever (meningitis)
- Sudden vision loss
- Unable to move limbs or sudden paralysis

### Trauma Emergencies
- **Severe head injury with loss of consciousness**
- Suspected spinal injury (don't move patient)
- Major burns (especially to face/airway)
- Penetrating injuries to chest/abdomen
- Fractures with bone visible or limb deformed
- Amputation or near-amputation

## HIGH PRIORITY - Urgent Medical Attention Needed

### Infection Red Flags
- Fever >40째C (104째F) especially with confusion
- Fever that doesn't respond to treatment
- High fever in infant <3 months
- Stiff neck with fever
- Rapidly spreading skin infection (red, warm, painful)
- Signs of sepsis: fever + fast heart rate + confusion

### Abdominal Red Flags
- Severe abdominal pain with rigid abdomen
- Vomiting blood or material like coffee grounds
- Black tarry stools or bright red blood in stool
- Severe abdominal pain in pregnant woman
- Inability to pass gas or stool with abdominal distension

### Pregnancy Red Flags
- Vaginal bleeding in pregnancy
- Severe headache or visual changes in late pregnancy
- Severe abdominal pain in pregnancy
- Decreased fetal movement
- Water breaking before 37 weeks
- Contractions before 37 weeks
- High blood pressure in pregnancy (pre-eclampsia signs)

### Pediatric Red Flags
- Bulging fontanelle in infant
- Inconsolable crying in infant
- Not feeding or drinking in infant
- Rash that doesn't blanch (meningococcal)
- Severe dehydration (sunken eyes, dry mouth, no tears)
- Very high fever in child with seizure
- Blue color around lips

### Mental Health Red Flags
- Active suicidal thoughts with plan
- Violent behavior toward self or others
- Severe psychosis with loss of reality
- Drug overdose
- Severe alcohol withdrawal (tremors, confusion)

## CAUTION - Medical Review Within 24 Hours

### General Warning Signs
- Fever lasting >3 days
- Unexplained weight loss
- Persistent vomiting (>24 hours)
- Blood in urine
- New lump that is growing
- Changing mole or skin lesion
- Persistent cough >3 weeks (TB screening needed)
- Night sweats with weight loss

### Specific Conditions
- Diabetic with very high or very low blood sugar
- Severe asthma attack not responding to inhaler
- Severe allergic reaction (swelling, hives, difficulty breathing)
- Eye injury or sudden vision changes
- Severe ear pain especially with fever

## RED FLAGS BY AGE GROUP

### Infants (0-12 months)
1. Temperature >38째C in baby <3 months
2. Not feeding for >8 hours
3. Fewer than normal wet diapers
4. Difficulty breathing or grunting
5. Extreme sleepiness or difficulty waking
6. Bulging soft spot on head
7. Inconsolable crying
8. Rash with fever

### Children (1-12 years)
1. Severe headache with neck stiffness
2. Rash that doesn't fade with pressure
3. Confusion or unusual behavior
4. Difficulty breathing
5. Refusing fluids for >12 hours
6. Severe abdominal pain
7. Blood in vomit or stool

### Adults
1. Chest pain with sweating/nausea
2. Sudden weakness or facial drooping
3. Sudden severe headache
4. High fever with confusion
5. Difficulty breathing at rest
6. Vomiting blood
7. Sudden vision loss

### Elderly (>65 years)
1. Falls with head injury
2. Confusion or sudden mental changes
3. Fever (may be lower threshold: >38째C)
4. Difficulty breathing
5. Chest pain (may present atypically)
6. Sudden weakness
7. Unable to pass urine

## ACTION GUIDE

### For CRITICAL Red Flags:
1. Call for help immediately
2. Keep airway open
3. Stop severe bleeding with pressure
4. Do not move if spinal injury suspected
5. Begin CPR if no breathing/pulse
6. Transport to facility URGENTLY

### For HIGH PRIORITY Red Flags:
1. Assess vital signs
2. Provide first aid as appropriate
3. Arrange transport to health facility
4. Do not delay - time matters

### For CAUTION Red Flags:
1. Document symptoms carefully
2. Advise medical review within 24 hours
3. Give safety net advice (when to return earlier)
4. Ensure follow-up plan
